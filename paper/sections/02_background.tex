\section{Background and Relation to Existing Standards}
\label{sec:background}

The representation of scientific and clinical evidence has been developed
across clinical informatics, biomedical ontology, and genomic knowledge
representation. Six standards families bear directly on our work: HL7 FHIR
Evidence~\cite{fhir_evidence}; the Evidence and Conclusion Ontology
(ECO)~\cite{eco2019} and the Scientific Evidence and Provenance Information
Ontology (SEPIO)~\cite{sepio}; the GA4GH Variant Annotation and Variation
Representation specifications~\cite{ga4gh_va,ga4gh_vrs}; the ACMG/AMP
variant-interpretation guidelines~\cite{acmg2015}; the upper ontologies IAO,
OBI, and PROV-O~\cite{iao2015,obi2016,prov_o}; and the ClinGen, MONDO, and HPO
curation and terminology resources~\cite{clingen2018,mondo,hpo}. Each captures
part of what a basic-science genetic publication reports, but none captures the
whole: a single conditional-knockout study may report molecular, cellular, and
clinical phenotypes at once, and a polygenic-score study may aggregate millions
of variants into one score. A per-family review, with the specific coverage
gaps that motivate our model, is given in Supplementary Note~S1. Of these
families, FHIR Evidence is the closest structural relative of our model, and we
position the model against it here.

\subsection{Relation to FHIR Evidence}
\label{sec:background-fhir}

FHIR is an HL7 information model rather than an OBO Foundry ontology, and a
genetic-evidence model could in principle be built directly on the OBO side.
Doing so first would be premature. The OBO evidence resources ECO and SEPIO
supply, respectively, an evidence-type vocabulary and a model of assertion
provenance and qualifiers, but neither supplies the variable-and-certainty
content model of an evidence unit that FHIR Evidence provides and that
basic-science evidence needs in order to be carried from bench to bedside.

Aligning a genetics-only model directly to OBO, before that general content
model exists, would yield a narrow resource: one that neither composes with
other basic-science domains nor reuses OBO's orthogonal vocabularies cleanly,
the redundancy that OBO's own emphasis on orthogonality and reuse exists to
prevent. We therefore take the structural content model from FHIR, generalise
it from clinical trials to basic science, and align the dimensions to ECO,
SEPIO, IAO, and OBI at the term level (initial mapping in Supplementary
Table~S2). Genetics is the first specialisation; broadening to other
basic-science domains and completing the OBO term alignment are future work for
other teams. Building the general content model first and the OBO-aligned
specialisation second is what keeps the eventual resource broad and reusable
rather than narrow.

The HL7 FHIR Evidence, EvidenceVariable, and EvidenceReport
resources~\cite{fhir_evidence} were designed around systematic-review and
guideline-development workflows in which evidence concerns an intervention
tested in a population. Like the other standards above, FHIR Evidence does not
represent the evidence produced by basic and pre-clinical research at all. Our
three core classes address exactly that gap. They are a basic-science model,
general across the basic sciences rather than specific to genetics: they keep
FHIR's separation of the evidence variable from the certainty of the finding,
fold FHIR's separate \texttt{statistic} into the variable (the measured value
is carried on the \EvidenceVariable{}), and drop FHIR's clinical-trial content
frame (population, intervention, comparator, outcome). Genetics is the worked
specialisation developed in this paper (\cref{sec:model}), in which
\ScientificEvidence{}, \EvidenceVariable{}, and \EvidenceAssertion{} specialise
to \GeneticEvidence{}, \GeneticEvidenceVariable{}, and
\GeneticEvidenceAssertion{}. \Cref{tab:fhir-alignment} gives the class-level
correspondence; the field-level alignment, including the certainty
subcomponents discussed in \cref{sec:model-dimensions}, is in Supplementary
Table~S2.
\footnote{Our \EvidenceAssertion{} is a structured, machine-checkable predicate, not the
FHIR \texttt{Evidence.assertion} element (a human-readable summary).
Structurally it belongs to the assertion-with-evidence family of the GA4GH VA
\texttt{Statement} and the SEPIO assertion; the meta-predicate layer it
supports~\cite{our_preprint} acts as a normative constraint over such
assertions rather than as another description of one, with term-level alignment
left to future work.}
