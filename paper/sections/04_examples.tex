\section{Illustrative Examples from the Literature}
\label{sec:examples}

To demonstrate the model's coverage and to see where it strains, we
applied it to six published genetic-evidence papers that span the
major epistemic shapes encountered in literature-based variant
interpretation (Supplementary Table~ST9). The six span the principal
epistemic shapes the model must accommodate: classical Mendelian human
genetics (Gupta), population-scale association (Duerr) and case--control
resequencing (Davis), model-organism and in vitro functional genetics
(Jossin, Nelson, and the functional arms of Davis), and a genome-wide
polygenic score (Inouye). Gupta is the canonical, schema-fitting case
the model was designed for, a small X-linked pedigree with clear
segregation that the schema accommodates without strain; Inouye is the
deliberate stress test, a whole-genome aggregate score the variant-level
model was not built for, and it surfaces the most candidate extensions
of any paper in the corpus. Four of the six were annotated
manually from curator PDF highlights and callouts and are treated as
ground truth. The remaining two were drafted by an AI assistant
working from the PDF text alone, with curator review serving as the
evaluation signal. All six annotations are available in the project
repository; here we discuss two in detail.

Duerr et al.\ 2006 (\cref{sec:ex-duerr}) serves as our reference
example of a classical variant-level association study. Inouye et
al.\ 2018 (\cref{sec:ex-inouye}) is a polygenic risk score study
whose annotation deliberately strains the model and surfaces a
cluster of candidate extensions (\cref{sec:discussion}). All six
annotations and per-paper case reports are in the repository's
\texttt{annotations/} and \texttt{case-reports/}
directories.\footnote{\url{https://github.com/ForomePlatform/genetic-evidence-model/tree/master/case-reports}}

\subsection{A Genome-Wide Association Study Example}
\label{sec:ex-duerr}

Duerr et al. 2006 identified IL23R as an inflammatory bowel disease gene through a genome-wide association study in
non-Jewish cases with ileal Crohn's disease, followed by independent replication and family-based transmission
testing. Under our decomposition the paper yields seven GeneticEvidence items (Supplementary Table~ST9 and Case
Report CR-DU): variant-level discovery and replication, a family-based transmission analysis, conditional
fine-mapping within IL23R, negative results at related IL-23 pathway genes, and cited functional and mouse-model
support. Six of the seven items fit the current schema without strain. The seventh, a cited anti-p40 antibody trial,
is drug-response evidence that exercises no existing genetic knowledge domain and therefore surfaces a
translational-scope gap. Curator review of this annotation also motivated three candidate extensions that remain
provisional under the two-papers rule: an explicit direction-of-effect dimension for protective vs risk-conferring
alleles, a construct for cross-item therapeutic synthesis, and an extension of the conditional-activation scope from
gene- to variant-level Mendelian context.

More details can be found in the Supplementary Note~SN6.1.


\subsection{A Polygenic Risk Score Stress Test}
\label{sec:ex-inouye}

Inouye et al. 2018 constructed and externally validated a meta-genomic risk score (metaGRS) for coronary artery
disease from 1,745,180 SNPs, combining three prior scores and testing performance in 482,629 UK Biobank
participants. Unlike Duerr's variant-level association, the target here is a derived score spanning the whole genome
in aggregate, and the main measurements are epidemiological (hazard ratio, C-index, cumulative incidence). Our
annotation decomposes the paper into five GeneticEvidence items covering score construction, external validation,
independence from conventional risk factors, age-stratified cumulative risk, and performance in medicated subgroups (
Case Report CR-IN). All five items are representable in the current model but only through deliberate ``least-wrong''
choices (for example, treating the score as a gene-level target). These forced fits are recorded as six candidate
extensions, including a score target type, a whole-genome aggregate resolution, a target-composition axis
distinguishing single from aggregate targets, an epidemiological measurement-target vocabulary, a structured cohort
descriptor, and a flag for derived-artifact targets. Where and how the model strains is thus made explicit rather
than hidden.

More details can be found in the Supplementary Note~SN6.2.
