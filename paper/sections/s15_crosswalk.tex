\section{Standards crosswalk}
\label{sec:supp-crosswalk}

This section gives the term-level crosswalk from the \GeneticEvidence{}
(GEM) constructs to external vocabularies, extending the class-level
FHIR alignment in the main paper down to the value level. Mappings to the
Sequence Ontology (SO), the Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO), NCBITaxon,
the Evidence and Conclusion Ontology (ECO), SEPIO, and the GA4GH Variant
Annotation (VA) model were verified against the source ontologies.
Throughout, the \emph{relation} of a GEM construct to an external term is
one of \emph{exact} (same scope), \emph{narrower} (GEM is more specific),
\emph{broader} (GEM is more general), or \emph{none} (no equivalent, a
coverage gap). The crosswalk is selective: it lists the dimensions with
external matches and the principled gaps, not every conditional field.

The property \texttt{assertion} and the class \EvidenceAssertion{} are
distinct despite their similar names. The property carries the
object-level, source-anchored claim a paper makes; its structural
analogues are the VA \texttt{Statement} and the SEPIO assertion, and
\emph{not} FHIR's \texttt{Evidence.assertion}, which is a human-readable
summary. The class \EvidenceAssertion{} is a meta-predicate, the model's
point of contact with the reasoning layer of~\cite{our_preprint}; no
current biomedical ontology represents predicates about predicates for
decision rules, so its lack of an external analogue is expected rather
than a failure. The property is slated to be renamed in a later schema
revision to remove the collision; the pilot annotations are unchanged.

Two observations follow. The dimensions describing \emph{what} the
evidence is about and \emph{in what organism} map exactly onto
established ontologies, and method maps cleanly for its three
experimental values; the single method gap is statistical genetics,
because ECO codes evidence types rather than study designs (GWAS,
linkage, and the like), for which OBI is the candidate vocabulary. The
remaining gaps, Knowledge Domain, Phenotype Scale, Variant Ascertainment,
Resolution, and the Gene Relation enumeration, are GEM-specific
epistemological axes without a single external term; they are coverage
gaps in the existing standards rather than mapping failures, and they are
part of what motivates a dedicated reference data model.
