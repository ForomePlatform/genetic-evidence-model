\section{Dimension coverage over the annotated corpus}
\label{sec:supp-coverage-note}

This note reports how often each dimension is populated across the 28
\GeneticEvidence{} items in the pilot corpus, and in how many
items each dimension is applicable under the conditional-activation
rules. The counts are produced by \fpath{scripts/compute_coverage.py}
from the released YAML annotations, and a per-paper report is provided in
the repository under
\fpath{annotations/coverage.md}.\footnote{\url{https://github.com/ForomePlatform/genetic-evidence-model/blob/master/annotations/coverage.md}}

The six always-required dimensions are 28 of 28 by construction, with one
exception: Knowledge Domain is 27 of 28, because a cited anti-p40
antibody trial in Duerr is drug-response evidence with no fitting genetic
knowledge domain. Among the conditional dimensions, Variant
Ascertainment (required when the target type is \dimval{Variant}) is 14
of 18: the four Inouye \texttt{v1} items force a \dimval{Variant} target
but leave ascertainment not-applicable (a polygenic score has no single
ascertainment mode), the CE-IN1 gap. Well-calibrated conditionals such as
Organism (8 of 8) and Measurement Target (10 of 11) are populated
whenever their activation fires, whereas Gene Relation (1 of 11) and
Knockout Type (1 of 8) expose enumeration and over-broad-activation gaps
that motivate candidate extensions. Mode of Inheritance and Mendelian
Segregation are 0 of 1: the single \dimval{Human Genetics}~$+$~\dimval{Gene}
item (Davis's case--control burden test) addresses them as not-applicable,
the activation condition firing more broadly than the dimension is
meaningful.
