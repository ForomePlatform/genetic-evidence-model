\section{Dimension coverage over the annotated corpus}
\label{sec:supp-coverage-note}

This note reports how often each dimension is populated across the 28
\GeneticEvidence{} items in the pilot corpus, and in how many
items each dimension is applicable under the conditional-activation
rules. The counts are computed from the released YAML annotations and
the SHACL shapes, and a machine-readable report is provided in the
repository under
\fpath{annotations/coverage.md}.\footnote{\url{https://github.com/ForomePlatform/genetic-evidence-model/blob/master/annotations/coverage.md}}

The first six always-required dimensions are 28 of 28 by construction,
with one exception: Knowledge Domain is 27 of 28, because a cited
anti-p40 antibody trial in Duerr is drug-response evidence with no
fitting genetic knowledge domain. Variant Ascertainment, required only
when the target type is \dimval{Variant}, is 21 of 25: the four Inouye
\texttt{v1} items force a \dimval{Variant} target but deliberately leave
ascertainment not-applicable (the polygenic score has no single
ascertainment mode), which is itself the CE-IN1 gap. Among the other
conditional dimensions, well-calibrated ones such as Measurement Target
(10 of 11) and Organism (8 of 8) are almost always populated when their
activation conditions fire, whereas others highlight activation or
enumeration gaps that motivate candidate extensions, as discussed in the
main text.
