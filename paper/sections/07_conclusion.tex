\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper has introduced a semantic model for genetic evidence from
the biomedical literature. The model comprises a compact hierarchy of
core classes, a dimensional vocabulary whose conditional-activation
rules demand only the dimensions a given piece of evidence requires,
and a SHACL encoding suitable for automated validation. It is
grounded in an open corpus of six annotated papers spanning human,
population, model-organism, and polygenic-score genetics, and is
accompanied by a collaborative workflow that keeps curator-authored
ground truth distinct from AI-drafted annotations and that promotes
candidate schema extensions only when a second independent paper
confirms them. Taken together, these elements are offered as a small
but concrete contribution to the ICBO 2026 focus on AI-ready,
ontologically grounded infrastructure: a step toward trustworthy
variant interpretation and toward a future in which parsing the
scientific literature is a routine, auditable activity rather than
a purely manual one.
