\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have introduced a semantic model for genetic evidence from the
biomedical literature: a compact hierarchy of core classes, a
dimensional vocabulary whose conditional-activation rules demand
only the dimensions a given piece of evidence requires, and a SHACL
encoding suitable for automated validation. The model is grounded
in an open corpus of six annotated papers and accompanied by a
collaborative workflow that keeps curator-authored ground truth
distinct from AI-drafted annotations and promotes candidate schema
extensions only when a second independent paper confirms them.
Taken together, these elements are offered as a small but concrete
contribution to the ICBO 2026 focus on AI-ready, ontologically
grounded infrastructure for trustworthy variant interpretation.
