\section{Executable skills}
\label{sec:supp-skills}

Because the term may be unfamiliar to readers outside agent-based AI
tooling, we describe briefly what we mean by an executable
\emph{skill}. A skill is a packaged, version-controlled bundle of
natural-language instructions together with any supporting resources,
such as reference files, templates, or worked examples, that an AI
assistant loads on demand to perform a defined task in a repeatable
way~\cite{anthropic_skills}. It is the executable counterpart of a
written protocol: where a protocol tells a human reader what a task
requires, a skill states the same requirements in a form an assistant
can apply directly. Concretely, a skill is a folder containing a
top-level instruction file (by convention \texttt{SKILL.md}) that
describes when the skill applies and how to carry the task out, plus
the resources the instructions refer to. The assistant reviews the
skills available to it, loads only the one relevant to the task at
hand, and follows its instructions, so behaviour is governed by the
versioned bundle rather than improvised for each session. The general
mechanism is not specific to one model or vendor, and a cross-vendor
specification for it is emerging;\footnote{\url{https://agentskills.io}}
the two skills accompanying this work are implemented as Claude Agent
Skills.

The annotation skill packages the annotation protocol, and the review
skill packages the review protocol (\cref{sec:supp-protocol-suite}), so
that the rules a human curator follows can be applied by an AI
assistant under curator supervision. Both are published with the
protocols in the project
repository.\footnote{\url{https://github.com/ForomePlatform/genetic-evidence-model/tree/master/skills}}
Their purpose here is reproducibility and reuse, not autonomy: as the
provenance note above records, every AI-drafted annotation and every
review verdict was adjudicated by the human curator, who remains the
authority for the recorded annotations.
